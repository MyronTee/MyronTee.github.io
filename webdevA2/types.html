Type of Watches

When classified based on their anatomy, there are 6 different types of watches. The watch anatomy will then decide how the watch functions and its battery lifespan.

These watches are the Quartz Watches, the Automatic Watches, the Mechanical Watches, the Kinetic Watches, the Eco-Drive Watches, and the Tough Solar Watches.

A Deeper Look

Quartz Watches

Quartz watches are one of the most commonly found watches these day as the quartz movement is both reliable and affordable.

How a Quartz Watch works

For a quartz watch, a battery-powered current will move through a quartz crystal, making it oscillate incredibly fast. This oscillation will then power a motor that moves the hands of the watch. However, the downside to quartz watches is that they require an occasional battery replacement (usually 2 - 3 years depending on the make and model).

Automatic, Mechanical & Kinetic Watches

Automatic and Kinetic watches both features a self-winding design, which means that the watch will keep running as long as its worn regularly. Mechanical watches on the other hand will need to be manually wound up regularly to keep running.

More About Wound-Up Watches

Watches that have Wound-Up movement are generally very accurate, as long as they are worn on a regular basis. Although, in general, they simply are not as accurate as quartz watches and should not be expected to keep time to the same degree of accuracy. However, it should be noted that an automatic watch can keep running longer than a kinetic or mechanical watch as it still has a rechargable inside while the other two don't.

Ecodrive and Tough Solar Watches

Both Ecodrive and Tough Solar Watches are known for their capability to keep itself running using solar power. This is because they both have small solar panels on the watch face which is used to charge the watch's battery.
